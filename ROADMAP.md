## Development Milestones

### Milestone 1: Vertical Slice (End of Phase 3)
**Goal**: One complete playable sequence demonstrating all core systems
- Isaac's room to therapy office
- Full dialogue with Dr. Chen
- Working freedom and trust systems
- Complete with art and audio

### Milestone 2: Alpha Release (End of Phase 4)
**Goal**: Complete game, rough around edges
- All content implemented
- All paths playable
- All endings accessible
- May have bugs and placeholder assets

### Milestone 3: Beta Release (Mid Phase 5)
**Goal**: Feature complete, polishing in progress
- All bugs fixed
- All assets finalized
- Balanced and tested
- Ready for public testing

### Milestone 4: Release Candidate (End of Phase 5)
**Goal**: Ready for release pending final checks
- Fully polished
- Extensively tested
- Performance optimized
- Documentation complete

### Milestone 5: Launch (Phase 6)
**Goal**: Public release
- Distributed on chosen platforms
- Marketing materials deployed
- Community engagement begins

## Resource Requirements

### Team (Ideal)
- **Game Designer**: 1 (overall vision and systems)
- **Programmer**: 1-2 (AGS scripting and systems)
- **Writer**: 1 (dialogue and narrative)
- **Artist**: 1-2 (sprites, backgrounds, UI)
- **Sound Designer**: 1 (music and effects)
- **Tester**: 2-3 (QA and feedback)

### Tools
- Adventure Game Studio (free)
- Graphics software (GIMP/Photoshop/Aseprite)
- Audio software (Audacity/FL Studio)
- Version control (Git/GitHub)
- Project management (Trello/Notion)

### Budget Considerations (if outsourcing)

**Note**: These are estimates for indie/hobbyist rates. Professional studio rates can be 2-5x higher.

- **Art assets**: $500-$3,000 (depends on style, complexity, number of assets)
  - Character sprites: $50-200 per character (indie artists)
  - Backgrounds: $100-300 per scene
  - UI elements: $200-500 total
- **Audio (music/SFX)**: $300-$2,000
  - Background music: $100-500 per track (5-7 tracks) from indie composers
  - Sound effects: $5-20 per effect or $200-400 for packs
- **Voice acting** (optional): $2,000-$10,000+
  - Professional voice actors: $300-500 per hour (industry standard)
  - Estimated 15-20 hours total for full voice work
  - Amateur/indie voice actors: $100-200 per character (budget option)
  - Recording/editing: $500-1,500
  - Note: Voice acting is optional; many successful narrative games use text-only
- **Marketing**: $200-$2,000
  - Trailer production: $300-800 (professional editing)
  - Press kit and promotional materials: $200-500
  - Ads (optional): $500+ depending on campaign
- **Distribution**: Minimal
  - itch.io: Free (with optional revenue share)
  - Steam: $100 one-time fee (if pursuing that platform)
  
**Budget Tiers**:
- **Minimal** (DIY everything): $100-500 (tools and distribution only)
- **Indie** (selective outsourcing): $2,000-$8,000
- **Professional** (full outsourcing): $10,000-$30,000+

## Risk Management

### Technical Risks
- **AGS limitations**: Mitigate by prototyping complex systems early
- **Performance issues**: Regular optimization passes
- **Save system bugs**: Extensive testing of save/load

### Creative Risks
- **Scope creep**: Strict adherence to design document
- **Narrative coherence**: Regular story reviews
- **Balance issues**: Iterative playtesting

### Resource Risks
- **Time constraints**: Prioritize core features
- **Budget limits**: Use free/affordable tools
- **Team availability**: Build flexible schedule

### Mitigation Strategies
- Regular milestone reviews
- Maintain feature priority list
- Keep communication clear
- Document everything
- Plan for contingencies

## Freedom-Focused Development

Every development decision should ask: "Does this serve the theme of freedom?"

- Mechanics should give players meaningful choices
- Story should explore different aspects of freedom
- Puzzles should reward creative thinking
- Design should respect player autonomy
- Experience should feel empowering, even when challenging

**The game is about developing freedom - and so is the development process itself.**
